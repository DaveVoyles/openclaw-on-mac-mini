💬 Step 1 — Join the Slack workspace

OpenClaw lives in Slack, a free messaging app available on your phone, tablet, or computer. You don't need to know anything about Slack — just follow these steps:

1
Create a free Slack account Go to slack.com and sign up with your email. It's completely free.
2
Join the family Slack workspace Use this link to join: Join DV OpenClaw on Slack (valid until May 17, 2026). Link expired? Email Dave at you@example.com or text him and he'll send a fresh one — takes 30 seconds.
3
Accept the invite & install the app Click the invite link in your email, then install Slack on your phone or computer for the best experience.
💡 Tip: The Slack mobile app works great on iPhone and Android. You can chat with OpenClaw anywhere!

🤖 Step 2 — Start chatting

Once you're in the Slack workspace, find the OpenClaw bot in the sidebar under "Apps" or "Direct Messages." Then just type naturally — like texting a friend!

Here are some things you can ask right away:

"What's the weather in Seattle today?"
"Give me 3 easy dinner ideas for tonight."
"Can you summarize this article for me?" [paste link]
"Write a birthday message for my nephew."
"What are the best places to visit in Portugal?"
💡 Tip: No special commands needed — just talk to it like you would a knowledgeable friend. OpenClaw remembers what you said earlier in the conversation, so you can ask follow-up questions!

✅ Step 4 — Get your updated document back

After OpenClaw reviews or edits your document, it will give you the updated version right in the chat. Here's how to get the best results:

1
Upload your file or paste your text Drag a file into the Slack chat, or copy-paste your text directly.
2
Ask for what you want Be specific: "Make this more concise," "Rewrite this for a 5-year-old," or "Fix the grammar."
3
Copy the result OpenClaw will reply with the updated text. Just copy it and paste it wherever you need it!
💡 Tip: You can ask for multiple rounds of edits. Just say "make it shorter" or "add a friendlier tone" and it will keep refining until you're happy!

🔗 Optional — Connect your personal accounts

You can connect your own Dropbox and Gmail so OpenClaw can read your files and emails privately. No one else can see your account — each person connects separately.

1
Connect Dropbox (one click) In Slack, type /clawbox connect. OpenClaw will send you a private link — just click it, sign in to Dropbox, and click Allow. Done!

Browse your Dropbox from Slack After connecting, type /clawbox list to see your files, or ask OpenClaw: "What's in my Dropbox?" — it can read, summarize, and analyze any file.

Connect Gmail (optional) Gmail setup is trickier — this step is completely optional. Skip it for now and come back any time. The rest of OpenClaw works great without it.
💡 This step is optional! OpenClaw works great without these connections. You can always set them up later.

📁 Step 5 — Let OpenClaw review your whole folder

Want OpenClaw to look at a folder of files on your own computer — like a work project, a folder of contracts, or a collection of notes? You have two options:

📎 Option A — Drag & drop in Slack ✅ Recommended

No install needed. Just drag files straight into the Slack chat and tell OpenClaw what to do with them. Works on any device, right now.

"Here's my report — can you tighten it up?" [drag file]
"Review all the docs in this zip and tell me which needs the most work." [drag zip]

Tip: For many files at once, select them all, compress into a zip, then drag the zip in.

🖥️ Option B — The OpenClaw CLI Advanced

Install a small helper app (one-time) and point it at any folder — OpenClaw reads the whole thing automatically. Great for large collections of files. Works on Mac and Windows.

Mac install — open Terminal and paste:

curl -fsSL https://openclaw.davevoyles.synology.me/install | bash

Windows install — open PowerShell and paste:

irm https://openclaw.davevoyles.synology.me/install.ps1 | iex

Then scan any folder:

cd ~/Desktop/MyDocs && openclaw scan
openclaw scan ~/Desktop/MyDocs "Suggest how to reorganize these files"
💡 Tip: Once installed, it reads your entire folder at once and sends everything to OpenClaw — like uploading a whole stack of papers in one go.

Not comfortable with this? That's totally fine — Option A works great for most people. You can always come back to this later when you're ready.
