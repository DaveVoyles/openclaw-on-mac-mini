💬 Step 1 — Join the Slack workspace

OpenClaw lives in Slack, a free messaging app available on your phone, tablet, or computer. You don't need to know anything about Slack — just follow these steps:

1
Create a free Slack account Go to slack.com and sign up with your email. It's completely free.
2
Tell Dave your Slack email Once you have an account, let Dave know the email you used and he'll send you an invite link to the workspace.
3
Accept the invite & install the app Click the invite link in your email, then install Slack on your phone or computer for the best experience.
💡 Tip: The Slack mobile app works great on iPhone and Android. You can chat with OpenClaw anywhere!

🤖 Step 2 — Start chatting

Once you're in the Slack workspace, find the OpenClaw bot in the sidebar under "Apps" or "Direct Messages." Then just type naturally — like texting a friend!

Here are some things you can ask right away:

"What's the weather in Seattle today?"
"Give me 3 easy dinner ideas for tonight."
"Can you summarize this article for me?" [paste link]
"Write a birthday message for my nephew."
"What are the best places to visit in Portugal?"
💡 Tip: No special commands needed — just talk to it like you would a knowledgeable friend. OpenClaw remembers what you said earlier in the conversation, so you can ask follow-up questions!

📄 Step 3 — Share a document for review

OpenClaw can read and work with your documents. You can share a file directly in Slack, or paste text into the chat. It works with:

📝

Word Docs

.docx files from Microsoft Word or Google Docs

📑

PDFs

Contracts, reports, articles — any PDF

📊

Spreadsheets

.xlsx Excel files and CSVs

✏️

Pasted Text

Copy-paste any text directly into the chat

After uploading or pasting, just tell OpenClaw what you need:

"Can you make this more concise?"
"Fix the grammar and make it sound more professional."
"Summarize the key points in plain English."
"What are the most important sections of this contract?"

✅ Step 4 — Get your updated document back

After OpenClaw reviews or edits your document, it will give you the updated version right in the chat. Here's how to get the best results:

1
Upload your file or paste your text Drag a file into the Slack chat, or copy-paste your text directly.
2
Ask for what you want Be specific: "Make this more concise," "Rewrite this for a 5-year-old," or "Fix the grammar."
3
Copy the result OpenClaw will reply with the updated text. Just copy it and paste it wherever you need it!
💡 Tip: You can ask for multiple rounds of edits. Just say "make it shorter" or "add a friendlier tone" and it will keep refining until you're happy!

📁 Step 5 — Review a whole folder of documents

Want OpenClaw to look at multiple files at once — like a whole project folder? Dave can set up a shared folder that OpenClaw has access to. Once it's set up, you can ask things like:

"List all the files in the shared folder."
"Read the file called 'proposal.docx' and give me a summary."
"Which documents in the folder mention budget?"
"Review all the files and tell me which one needs the most editing."

To get access to the shared folder:

1
Ask Dave to add you Dave can give you access to a shared folder (called /ai-files) on his system.
2
Place files in the folder Copy your documents into the shared folder — Dave can help you do this the first time.
3
Ask OpenClaw to work with them In Slack, just tell OpenClaw the file name and what you need. It will read the file and respond!
💡 Tip: This is great for reviewing contracts, recipes, travel plans, or any set of documents you want AI help with all at once.
