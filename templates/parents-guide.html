OpenClaw is your personal AI assistant. Think of it like Gemini or ChatGPT, but it lives on your family's home server and is just for you.

You can use it two ways: from any browser, or through Slack.

This guide is for you — no technical knowledge needed.

🚀 New here? Start with the interactive setup guide — it walks you through everything in 5 easy steps.

🌐 Option 1: Use it from your browser (easiest)

  1. Open any web browser (Chrome, Safari, Edge — anything works)
  2. Go to: chat.davevoyles.synology.me
  3. Start typing — it works just like Gemini or ChatGPT

That's it. You can bookmark that page and go back to it any time.

Tip: On your first visit, you may see a login screen. If you need a username and password, ask Dave.

💬 Option 2: Use it from Slack

If you have Slack installed, you can chat with OpenClaw directly via a private Direct Message — no channels needed.

  1. Open Slack
  2. In the left sidebar, find @OpenClaw under Direct Messages
  3. Type your question and press Enter — OpenClaw will reply privately, just to you

You can also use slash commands from any channel:

/chat what does this word mean?

📲 Don't have Slack yet? Download it free for Mac, Windows, iPhone, or Android — or use it in your browser at app.slack.com. Then join the family workspace here (link valid until May 17, 2026 — ask Dave if expired).

👉 New to Slack? See Getting Started with Slack at the bottom of this page — it covers everything from downloading the app to sending your first message.

📄 Working with Word documents

  1. In Slack, open a DM with @OpenClaw
  2. Drag your Word file (.docx or .doc) into the chat window and drop it
  3. OpenClaw will ask what you'd like to do — just reply with your answer

Common things to say:

  • proofread this for me
  • make this sound more professional
  • summarize this in a few bullet points
  • fix the grammar

📊 Working with Excel spreadsheets

  1. Drag your Excel file (.xlsx or .xls) into Slack
  2. OpenClaw will ask what you'd like — reply with your question

Common things to say:

  • what is this tracking?
  • explain what column B means
  • are there any errors or unusual numbers?
  • write me a short summary of this

💡 Tips for getting plain, easy-to-read answers

By default, OpenClaw gives detailed answers. If you'd prefer short, plain answers with no jargon:

Turn on simple mode once and forget about it:

From then on, all your answers will be short and easy to read — no technical language.

To turn it off:

/simple off

📁 Sync your Documents folder (Mac only)

Instead of dragging files into Slack every time, you can set up automatic syncing. Once installed, any Word or Excel file you drop into a special folder on your Mac will appear in OpenClaw within 30 seconds — no more uploads needed.

How it works

  1. You drop a file into ~/Documents/OpenClaw/ on your Mac
  2. OpenClaw picks it up automatically (within ~30 seconds)
  3. You open Slack, DM @OpenClaw, and just say what you want:
    • edit my report.docx
    • proofread budget.xlsx
    • summarize the letter I just dropped in

One-time setup

Ask Dave to help set this up — he has an installer script that handles everything automatically. Once done, just drop files in the folder and OpenClaw picks them up.

💡 Not comfortable with Terminal commands? That's fine — just ask Dave. He can set this up for you in about 2 minutes, remotely.

Supported file types

TypeExtensionMax size
Word documents.docx50 MB
Excel spreadsheets.xlsx50 MB

Troubleshooting

  • File didn't sync? Wait 30 seconds and try dropping it again. If still nothing, ask Dave.
  • Want to stop syncing: Ask Dave — he can turn it off in under a minute.

🔍 Research for your documents

OpenClaw can search the web for information and pull it right into your document — without you leaving Slack.

How to use it

Just type your research request in a DM to @OpenClaw:

research climate change for my annual report

or

look up tips for saving money on groceries

OpenClaw will:

  1. Search the web using Perplexity AI and gather up-to-date facts
  2. If you have an active document (see the /files tip below), it will suggest new paragraphs you can paste straight in
  3. Post everything in Slack so you can copy what you need

Tip: Use /files report.docx to set an active document first, then type your research request — OpenClaw will tailor the findings to your document automatically.

Keywords that trigger research:

  • research [topic]
  • look up [topic]
  • find info on [topic]
  • search for [topic]

You can also use the slash command from anywhere in Slack:

/research electric vehicles for my sustainability report

📦 Process multiple files at once

You can drop two or more files into a Slack message and OpenClaw will handle them all — one after another — with a live progress report in the thread.

How to use it

  1. In a DM with @OpenClaw, drag two or more Word or Excel files into the chat at the same time and drop them
  2. OpenClaw will start a thread showing progress for each file:
    • ⏳ 1/3: report.docx...
    • ✅ 1/3: report.docx done
    • ⏳ 2/3: budget.xlsx...
    • ✅ 2/3: budget.xlsx done
    • ...
  3. When all files are done, you'll see: ✅ All 3 files processed!

Batch with a slash command

If you've already uploaded files, you can run a batch action on all of them at once:

/batch summarize

Other actions you can use with /batch:

  • /batch proofread — proofread all files
  • /batch explain — explain each file in plain language

Note: Processing multiple large files takes a little longer — OpenClaw pauses briefly between each file to stay reliable.

If things feel confused (OpenClaw keeps referencing an old file, or the conversation got muddled), just type:

This resets your active file selections and clears any in-progress comparison or translation. Your file history stays intact — /clear just gives you a clean slate for your next question.

📬 Daily File Digest — /digest

If you want a daily summary of files that have been synced to OpenClaw, turn on the digest:

Each day, OpenClaw will send you a private message listing any new files that arrived in the last 24 hours — file names, types, and upload times.

By default, your daily digest arrives at whatever time the server happens to check. Want it at a specific time each day? Just tell OpenClaw when:

🔗 Connecting Your Accounts

OpenClaw can connect to your own Gmail and Dropbox accounts. You set this up yourself with a quick command in Slack — no need to ask Dave. Each person connects their own account, so Chuck's email stays Chuck's and Lisa's stays Lisa's.

📧 Connect Your Gmail — /email setup

Step 1 — Turn on 2-Step Verification (if not already on):

  1. Go to myaccount.google.com/security
  2. Scroll to "How you sign in to Google" and enable 2-Step Verification

Step 2 — Create an App Password:

  1. Go to myaccount.google.com/apppasswords
  2. Sign in if asked
  3. Under "App name" type OpenClaw, then click Create
  4. Google shows you a 16-character password like abcd efgh ijkl mnop — copy it

Step 3 — Connect in Slack:

/email setup yourname@gmail.com abcdefghijklmnop

(paste your Gmail address and the app password — no spaces in the password)

That's it! OpenClaw will confirm it's connected.

Using /email once connected:

/email                    — shows your last 10 emails
/email week               — emails from the past 7 days
/email doctor             — search for emails about your doctor
/email insurance          — find anything about insurance

To disconnect your Gmail:

/email forget

📅 Check Your Calendar — /calendar

View your Google Calendar without opening the app:

/calendar                 — shows today's events
/calendar today           — same as above
/calendar week            — what's coming up this week

📦 Connect Your Dropbox — /clawbox connect

💡 Note: The command is /clawbox — that's our name for your Dropbox connection. Slack reserves the name "/dropbox" so we use "/clawbox" instead. It's the same thing!

Connecting your Dropbox is now one click — no developer accounts needed.

Just type this in Slack:

/clawbox connect

OpenClaw will send you a private link. Click it, sign into your Dropbox account, click Allow, and you're done. OpenClaw will DM you a confirmation.

🔒 The link is private — only you can see it, and it expires in 10 minutes.

Using /clawbox once connected:

/clawbox list             — list files in your OpenClaw folder
/clawbox status           — check if your Dropbox connection is working

How the automatic watcher works:

Once connected, whenever you drop a file into the OpenClaw folder in your Dropbox, OpenClaw will message you in Slack within 30 seconds:
"New file detected! Want me to summarise it?"
Just say yes and OpenClaw reads it for you.

To disconnect your Dropbox:

/clawbox forget

💻 Windows: Sync Without Extra Software

If you're on Windows and can't use the SSH sync method, OpenClaw now supports uploading files directly over the network. The Windows installer (Install-OpenClaw.ps1) automatically tests this connection during setup.

If you see "✅ Upload server reachable" during install, the HTTP fallback is working and your files will sync even without additional tools installed.

💬 Getting Started with Slack

Slack is a messaging app — think of it like iMessage or WhatsApp, but organized for a family or small group. It's free and works on any device. Here's everything you need to know to get comfortable with it.

Download Slack

Download it free for your device:

  • Android
  • Or use it directly in your browser at app.slack.com — no download required

Channels vs. Direct Messages — what's the difference?

Channels are group chats where everyone in the family can see the conversation. You'll spot them in the left sidebar with names like #general. Think of it like a shared family bulletin board.

Direct Messages (DMs) are private, one-on-one conversations — only you and the other person can see them. This is where you talk to OpenClaw.

✅ You do not need to use a channel to talk to OpenClaw. Always use a Direct Message — it's private, instant, and keeps your conversation tidy.

How to find and message OpenClaw

  1. Open Slack on your device
  2. Look at the left sidebar — scroll down to the Direct Messages section
  3. Click on OpenClaw (it may show as @OpenClaw or with a 🐾 icon)
  4. Type anything in the message box at the bottom and press Enter
  5. OpenClaw will reply within a few seconds

If you don't see OpenClaw in the list, click the + next to Direct Messages and search for OpenClaw.

What are slash commands?

Slash commands are shortcuts that start with a / (forward slash). You type them directly in the message box and press Enter — no need to write a full sentence.

Examples you'll see throughout this guide:

  • /help — shows everything OpenClaw can do
  • /simple on — switches to shorter, plain-English answers
  • /brief — shows a summary of your recent files
  • /chat what does this mean? — ask a quick question from any channel

Whenever you see something in this guide starting with /, just type it into Slack and press Enter.

How to send a file to OpenClaw

You can share Word documents, Excel spreadsheets, and PDFs right in the chat — just like attaching something to an email:

  1. Open your DM with OpenClaw
  2. Drag a file from your desktop and drop it into the chat window
    (or click the + button next to the message box and choose a file)
  3. Press Enter to send it
  4. OpenClaw will ask what you'd like to do — just reply in plain English

Tips for new Slack users

  • You can't break anything. Just try it — if something doesn't work, type /help.
  • OpenClaw only sees your direct messages to it. It does not read other channels or anyone else's conversations.
  • Your conversation is saved. Scroll up in the DM at any time to see past questions and answers.
  • The mobile app is great for quick questions. Download Slack on your phone and you can ask OpenClaw anything from anywhere.
  • No typing needed for short questions. Just write naturally — "what does this letter mean?" works perfectly.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Is my conversation private?

Yes. OpenClaw runs on Dave's home server — not a public cloud. Only people Dave has invited can access it, and your direct messages are only visible to you. OpenClaw does not share your conversations with anyone.

Does OpenClaw remember me between sessions?

Yes — it keeps a short memory of your recent conversations so it can follow along if you refer to something earlier. However, very old messages may drop out of its context. If you notice it seems to have forgotten something important, just mention it again.

Can I use it on my phone?

Absolutely. Two easy options:

  • Slack mobile app — download Slack for iPhone or Android, sign in, and message OpenClaw just like on a computer.
  • Browser — open Safari or Chrome and go to chat.davevoyles.synology.me. Works on any phone.

What if it gives a wrong answer?

OpenClaw is an AI — it can occasionally make mistakes, especially on facts it wasn't specifically trained on. Always double-check important information (health, legal, financial decisions) with a professional source. If an answer seems off, just ask it to try again: "Are you sure about that?" or "Can you double-check that?"

What can OpenClaw not do?

  • It cannot browse the live internet (unless you ask it to search — and even then it may have limits).
  • It cannot make phone calls, send emails on your behalf (unless you've connected Gmail), or access accounts you haven't linked.
  • It does not know about events after its training cutoff date.
  • It cannot control other apps on your phone or computer.

💡 Rule of thumb: Use OpenClaw for questions, summaries, drafting text, and explaining things. For anything you'd normally call a professional about, use it as a starting point — not the final word.

What if OpenClaw stops responding?

The home server may be restarting — this happens occasionally after updates and usually takes less than 2 minutes. Just wait a moment and try again. If OpenClaw is still unresponsive after 5 minutes, send Dave a text and he'll check the server.

What happens to my uploaded files?

Your files are stored privately on Dave's home server — they're not uploaded to any public cloud or shared service. Only you can see your files. You can ask OpenClaw to delete a file at any time: just say "delete that file" or "clear my history." Files are not kept forever — old files are automatically cleaned up after a period of inactivity.
